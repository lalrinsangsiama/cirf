Component Combinations and Their Failure Rates

Calculation Methodology

How Failure Rates Are Calculated

Based on analysis of 362 cultural innovation cases, failure rates are calculated for specific component combinations:

Failure Rate = (Closed Cases + Crisis Cases) / Total Cases with Combination × 100% Where: - Closed Cases = Enterprises that have ceased operations - Crisis Cases = Enterprises experiencing significant challenges - Total Cases = All cases exhibiting the specific component combination

Example Calculation: Cultural Integrity WITHOUT Economic Value

Step 1: Identify cases with Cultural Integrity = 1 AND Economic Value = 0
Step 2: Count total cases with this combination: 45 cases
Step 3: Count failed/crisis cases: 32 cases (24 closed + 8 crisis)
Step 4: Calculate failure rate: 32/45 × 100% = 71.1%

Critical Insights from Failure Analysis

The Fatal Combinations

Three combinations show >80% failure rate:

  1. Cultural Preservation without Economics (89%): Pure cultural focus without revenue model
  2. Economic Extraction without Community (82%): External profit without local benefit
  3. Static Operations without Adaptation (76%): Good today, obsolete tomorrow
The Missing Link Pattern

Most failures result from missing "bridge components" that connect different aspects:

  • Sustainable Development: Bridges economic and community dimensions
  • Adaptive Capacity: Bridges current operations and future viability
  • Community Benefit: Bridges cultural practice and local support
The 3-2-1 Rule for Avoiding Failure

Analysis reveals a minimum viable configuration:

  • 3 Operational Pillars (must include Economic + one other)
  • 2 Community Control Filters (must include Benefit Assessment)
  • 1 Resilience Capacity (preferably Adaptive)

This configuration shows only 23% failure rate compared to 71% for incomplete configurations.
