Research Finding

4 Dimensions of Heritage
as Economic Shield

01
Identity & Community Cohesion
Heritage transmits values across generations, fostering belonging and unity that strengthens crisis response.
02
Social Capital
Cultural practices build trust networks and cooperation between groups, creating mutual aid systems for recovery.
03
Economic Engine
Cultural tourism, craftsmanship, and heritage festivals generate income and jobs, diversifying local economies.
04
Environmental Knowledge
Traditional practices like regenerative agriculture inform sustainable approaches to climate adaptation.
Source: Musialik, W. et al. (2025). "Cultural heritage capital as a resilience factor in sustainable development policy of the region." Economics and Environment.
